I am a software engineer and computer science teacher in Colorado.

I spent four years at NetApp writing C and C++ for enterprise storage firmware — reading unfamiliar subsystems, isolating bugs in multi-threaded code, and shipping fixes through agile review cycles. Then I left to get a master's degree in education and became a CS teacher at a small project-based school in Commerce City.

I teach Python, AP Computer Science A (Java), and engineering design. I also built Quill, a personal project — a React/TypeScript frontend over a Python/Flask/SQLite backend — mostly because I needed it to exist and no one else was going to build it.

These essays are my attempt to think clearly about what I have learned in both roles: how debugging is a discipline you can teach, how complexity in code mirrors complexity in a classroom, and what it means to understand something well enough to explain it to someone else.
